How to Reply to “Per Our Conversation” Professionally

“Per our conversation” is commonly used to document a verbal discussion in writing. It creates a paper trail to ensure shared understanding of decisions, tasks, or agreements.

Replying is often essential. Your response confirms accuracy, corrects mistakes, or formally acknowledges the summary to avoid future disputes.

Copy & Paste Replies

Neutral

Received. I will proceed with the next steps as discussed.

Client-Safe

Thank you for the summary. I’m aligned with these points and will keep you updated.

Internal Team

Got it. I’m working on this now.

Formal

I confirm that this summary accurately reflects our discussion. Please proceed.

Clarifying

Thanks for this summary. I’d like to clarify one detail regarding the timeline.

Closing

Perfect, thanks for documenting this.

When You Should Reply

  • The email assigns tasks or responsibilities to you.
  • You notice an error that could cause confusion later.
  • The message documents a formal or contractual agreement.

When You Should Not Reply

  • The email is informational and requires no confirmation.
  • You already acknowledged the details elsewhere.
  • You were CC’d only for visibility.
