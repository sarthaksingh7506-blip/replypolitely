How to Apologize for a Late Email

When a response is delayed, long explanations do more harm than good. A brief acknowledgment followed by action shows professionalism and respect for the recipient’s time.

Copy & Paste Replies

Brief

Apologies for the delay in getting back to you. Here is the information you requested.

Professional

Thank you for your patience. I wanted to confirm the details before responding.

Client-Safe

Thank you for your patience while I looked into this. The issue is now resolved and you can proceed.

Internal Team

Sorry for the delay — I was heads-down on the launch. Reviewing this now.

Action-Oriented

Apologies for the delay. I’ve attached the updated file and am available this afternoon if you’d like to review.

When to Apologize

  • • The response took more than a business day
  • • The sender followed up
  • • The delay affected someone else’s timeline

When to Move On

  • • The delay was minor
  • • The thread is informal
  • • You already acknowledged it once
