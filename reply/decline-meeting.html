How to Decline a Meeting Politely

Not every meeting is necessary. Declining thoughtfully protects your time without damaging trust. The goal is to be respectful, clear, and outcome-focused—not dismissive.

Copy & Paste Replies

Time Conflict

Thanks for the invite. I have a conflict at that time. Would it be possible to move this to Thursday afternoon?

Not Required

Thanks for including me. Based on the agenda, I don’t think my input is needed, so I’ll step out to focus on other priorities.

Delegate

I won’t be able to attend, but I’ve looped in Sarah, who’s closer to the details and can represent our team.

Asynchronous Option

I can’t commit to a full meeting, but I’m happy to review the materials and send feedback by end of day.

Firm Decline

I’ll need to decline as I’m focused on a critical deadline this week. Please flag any urgent action items for me.

When to Decline

  • The meeting has no clear agenda
  • Your role is undefined
  • It conflicts with high-priority work

When to Attend

  • You are a key decision-maker
  • Your presence unblocks others
  • The relationship is new or sensitive
