How to Reply to “As Per Your Request” Professionally

“As per your request” is commonly used when someone delivers information, files, or work you specifically asked for. It signals task completion and a handoff back to you.

Your reply should confirm receipt and clearly indicate the next step—whether that is review, approval, feedback, or closure.

Copy & Paste Replies

Neutral

Received, thank you for sending this over.

Client-safe

Thank you. I will review this shortly and follow up if anything is needed.

Internal team

Got it. Thanks for the quick turnaround.

Formal

I acknowledge receipt of the requested materials. We will proceed with the review.

Clarifying

Thanks. Can you confirm this includes the latest revisions we discussed?

Closing

Perfect, this is exactly what I needed. Thank you.

When You Should Reply

  • You requested the item and need to confirm receipt.
  • The sender requires approval to proceed.
  • You want to maintain a professional client relationship.

When You Should Not Reply

  • The message is an automated system delivery.
  • You were copied for reference only.
  • No follow-up action is required.
